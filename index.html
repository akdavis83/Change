Change

(Philippians 3:21) Who shall change our vile body, that it may be fashioned like unto his glorious body, according to the working whereby he is able even to subdue all things unto himself.

Suffering is univeral to all of humanity. When we see someone suffering, especially for a long time, it is natural to want to give comfort and relieve the suffering. Pain, malodies, infirmities, ailments, sickness, malfunction, these are a few of the things that we all go through while in a mortal body. The gosepl of Jesus Christ offers a solution that is above what doctors can bring for when there is no natural hope left through healing virtue a miracle can occur. But even a miracle has a limitation to it, in that we cannot make them happen, we cannot determine who gets healed and who does not, that is beyond our ability. All we can do is believe for a complete recovery and leave the rest to the Lord Jesus Christ. When we lack power to bring about the desired changes that we personally want to see occur, if the desperation for change is great enough, brute force is the only other option. The overthrow of bad governments, abortions for people who made a mistake and do not want to live with the consequences are just a few examples of what this could look like. The next quasi-evolutionary leap for mankind is leaning towards the changing of our bodies. The push will be to live longer by any means necessary while God has put a time limit on our mortal frames. And the LORD said, My spirit shall not always strive with man, for that he also is flesh: yet his days shall be an hundred and twenty years. Now there are some exceptions to that, some people live longer than that but not by much. There is nothing wrong with wanting to live a long, healthy life, it is a desire common to all. But if we just want to live longer to escape the judgement, to escape having to make a decision for Jesus Christ, that is another story altogether. The better off people think they are without Jesus, the less of a need they see to receive Him. The sole purpose of this life is not for personal gain and gratification. The purpose of this life is not to escape suffering and pain. These also are natural desires but if that is all there is then even the longest life is left void and empty. As society continues to cast off all restraint both morally and religious, the decline will become greater and greater. If we look with a limited lense it tends to become obscured and distorted, even too nearsighted. But when we zoom out a little bit and look from an eternal perspective we begin to see the bigger picture. Eternity is in our hearts, it is something we all long for and that is the drive behind wanting to live better and longer. It is a documented and historical fact that Christ was buried and rose again. When He rose from the dead by the power of the Holy Spirit He was given a new, glorious body. No longer flesh and blood, He was flesh and bones, the first of it's kind, a spiritual body that has a tangible, physical form. The same body He has will be given to all who recieve Him as Lord and Savior. This is the answer, this is the solution, this is the permanent change that lasts for eternity. 1 Corinthians 15:52 says: In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed. We want the fullness now and faster, and even though from the moment of spiritual birth the Spirit is working on changing us, the change is slow and blurry at best. We see people transformed from their former way of living but the process on earth is still incomplete. We give our hearts and lives to Jesus but we still see our own flaws, and though we see how far we have come, we also see an even further way to go. 2 Cor 3:18 says: But we all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord. Whether you need physical healing, a creative miracle, a renewed mind, or deliverance from evil spirits, the healing virtue is available and He is willing, but it first starts with the ultimate healing: Salvation in Christ!
